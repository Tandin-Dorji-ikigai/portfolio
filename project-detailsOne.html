About Vide

Vide is a UX case study and prototype for a modern digital art marketplace that empowers both art buyers and emerging artists. The platform was designed to address key usability challenges in the online art-buying experience, such as poor filtering, lack of artist visibility, and complex purchase flows.

The user-centric interface allows buyers to intuitively browse curated collections, filter artworks by style, medium, price, and size, and easily evaluate pieces with transparent artist bios and pricing. On the artist side, Vide simplifies the onboarding process and gives visibility through features like artist spotlights and a clean portfolio submission flow.

Problem Statement

In the current digital art landscape, art buyers often face difficulty in discovering original artworks that align with their taste, budget, and aesthetic. Traditional online art platforms can feel overwhelming, lack intuitive navigation, or don’t offer enough filtering and categorization to help users make informed choices. Furthermore, artists struggle to showcase their work to a global audience without complex onboarding processes.

Vide aims to bridge this gap by offering a seamless, curated, and highly visual shopping experience for original artworks. It allows users to easily browse, filter, and purchase artworks while giving artists a professional platform to feature and sell their creations.

Goals

The goal of Vide is to deliver an intuitive and inspiring user experience for browsing and purchasing art by empowering users with advanced filtering options such as category, style, subject, medium, and price. It also aims to support emerging artists by increasing their visibility and to ensure a secure, seamless checkout process for both collectors and first-time buyers.

Research Overview

User Behavior & Preferences: Users want a simple, aesthetically pleasing interface with robust filtering to discover relevant artworks. They also seek trust in pricing and artist reputation.

Market Analysis: Many art platforms focus either on luxury collectors or casual browsers, lacking a middle-ground experience that supports both audiences.

Competitor Study: Platforms like Saatchi Art and Artsy offer broad collections but often lack simplified user experiences and transparent filtering systems. Vide aims to offer a minimal yet powerful user flow with highly curated content.
